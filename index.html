The idea

Not another photo app. A way of seeing.

finds rewards what most apps miss: the world right in front of you.

You photograph the things people walk past — the rust-eaten sign, the perfect fallen leaf, the corner shop closing after sixty years. Every find is anchored to a place, the moment you saw it, and one of seven philosophical lenses.

No filters. No selfies. No algorithms deciding what matters. Just observation, made shareable.

Seven lenses

A vocabulary for noticing.

Every find belongs to one collection. The lens isn't a tag — it's a way of framing why this thing was worth stopping for.

01

Rare & bizarre

Genuinely weird, genuinely rare — things most people never see.

02

Everyday beauty

A satisfying shadow, a perfect reflection — not rare, just noticed.

03

Hyperlocal

What makes a place itself — the corner sign, the landmark tree.

04

Before it’s gone

Closing shops, old signs, things being demolished — documentation as care.

05

Patterns

Same tile in two cities — the pleasure of noticing connections across distance.

06

Human traces

Worn paths, notes on lampposts — left behind, unseen.

07

Overlooked ordinary

Not weird — just unnoticed. Attention is the point.

Discovery

Every find lives somewhere.

A living map of what people have noticed — near you, or across the world.

Photo bubbles ringed in each collection's color mark every discovery. Walk a familiar street and find what others saw that you missed. Travel somewhere new and follow the trail of curious eyes.

Because a find without a place is just a photograph.
